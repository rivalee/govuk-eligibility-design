Universal Credit

More information

You may be able to get help to pay your living costs if you’re on a low income. You could get Universal Credit if you’re working (including self-employed or part time), out of work or cannot work.

If you're already getting any benefits or tax credits, do not claim Universal Credit until you’ve checked that it will make you better off. This is because some benefits will stop as soon as you make a Universal Credit claim, even if it’s not successful.

Check if you’re eligible for Universal Credit

Jobseekers Allowance

More information

You could apply for 'new style' Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) to help you when you’re looking for work.

Check if you’re eligible for Jobseekers Allowance

Employment and Support Allowance (if sick)

More information

You can apply for Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) if you have a disability or health condition that affects how much you can work.

Check if you’re eligible for Employment and Support Allowance

Statutury Sick Pay (if sick)

More information

You can get £109.40 per week Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) if you’re too ill to work. It’s paid by your employer for up to 28 weeks.

Check if you’re eligible for Statutory Sick Pay

Carer's Allowance

More information

You could get £76.75 a week if you care for someone at least 35 hours a week and they get certain benefits.

Check if you’re eligible for Carer's Allowance

Carer's Credit

More information

You could get Carer’s Credit if you’re caring for someone for at least 20 hours a week.
